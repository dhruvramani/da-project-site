Dataset Details

 

    Feature Definition
    Consumer Price Index (CPI) Measures the change in the price of a specific group of goods and services and is commonly used to measure the level of inflation.
    Interest Rate It is the fee paid to the lender or investor for borrowing or investing funds, usually expressed as a percentage, reflecting the cost or return on funds.
    Inflation It is the sustained increase in the general price level in an economy over a period of time, resulting in a decrease in the purchasing power of money.
    Exchange Rate It is the price at which one currency can be exchanged for another.
    GDP Per Capita It is the total economic output of a country divided by its entire population and is used to measure the level of economic production per average citizen.
    Debt held by the Public It is the borrowed and outstanding debt owed to others by governments, corporations, or individuals and held by investors, banks, or other creditors.
    Intra-governmental Holdings It is the debt which the government owes and controls a portion of a domestic enterprise or institutionâ€™s equity, usually to exert influence over its operations and policy direction or to achieve public policy objectives.
    Total Public Debt Outstanding It is the cumulative amount of all borrowing and debt, including bonds and other forms of debt, that the Government still needs to repay.
    Government Gross Debt It is the total debt the government owes to various creditors and usually includes bonds, loans, and other borrowings used to finance government expenditures and programs.
    Stock market data (S&P and Nikkei 225) It is the exchange of shares and stocks by people who represent ownership of various companies.
    Date Date of collecting the data.
    News text data (From BBC data) Scraped data from BBC news API for both USA and Japan.
