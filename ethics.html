Ethical Considerations

  • Data Privacy: We need to ensure that we have the legal right to use the requested data and respect data privacy laws and regulations. In addition, we should protect sensitive information.
  • Real Time: Since the exchange rate is not a stable value, on the contrary, it is a data that changes in real time. Making it especially important to ensure real-time data in our project.
  • Transparency: We should be able to provide transparency in the methods of data collection, processing and analysis. Disclosing possible biases or limitations in the research is also a good step.
  • Avoiding Manipulation: When dealing with relevant data, we need to avoid manipulating the data or results, and to be able to present findings accurately and objectively, even if contrary to expectations.
  • Open Science and Reproducibility: Promoting open science practices by sharing data, code, and methods to promote reproducibility, and encourage peer review and collaboration to enhance the robustness of our work.
  • Continuous Monitoring and Feedback: Regularly monitor and reevaluate our programs with ethical considerations in order to achieve the better results. Moreover, we should be open to feedback and adapt our approach to ethical issues raised by our peers or community.
